# AI Incident Registry Governance

The AI Incident Registry is maintained as a public good for the global software development community. To ensure it remains neutral, objective, and resistant to vendor capture, the registry operates under this independent governance model.

## 1. Independence and Neutrality
While initially built and sponsored by Korext, the registry operates independently. No single vendor, including Korext, may dictate the inclusion, redaction, or alteration of an incident report outside the established review and dispute processes.

## 2. The Maintainer Committee
The registry is governed by a Maintainer Committee responsible for:
- Reviewing and approving incident submissions.
- Managing the taxonomy and YAML specification.
- Adjudicating disputes.

To enforce neutrality, **no single organization may employ more than 40% of the active maintainers.** We actively recruit security researchers, independent auditors, and engineers from diverse technical backgrounds to serve as maintainers.

## 3. Decision Process
- **Submissions**: Incident submissions require approval from at least one Maintainer.
- **Specification Changes**: Updates to `SPEC.md` or `TAXONOMY.md` require a pull request and approval from a majority of the active Maintainer Committee.
- **Disputes**: Incident disputes (e.g., a vendor challenging a report) require review by at least three independent Maintainers who must reach a consensus on whether to retain, redact, or withdraw the report based entirely on technical merit and the stated Ethical Commitments.

## 4. Vendor Participation
AI Tool Vendors are encouraged to participate by registering for notifications and engaging in the 14-day comment period when an incident involves their tool. However, vendors cannot demand the deletion of a factually accurate report merely because it presents their tool negatively. All decisions are based on technical accuracy and the `ETHICS.md` framework.

## 5. Amendments
This Governance model may be amended only by a two-thirds supermajority vote of the Maintainer Committee.
