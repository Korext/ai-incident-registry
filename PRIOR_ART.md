# Prior Art Analysis: AI Incident Registry vs Existing Databases

The AI Incident Registry complements, rather than replaces, existing industry databases. To ensure our standard is properly scoped and authoritative, we've analyzed the current landscape of vulnerability and incident databases.

## Existing Databases

### CVE (Common Vulnerabilities and Exposures)
- **Maintainer**: MITRE
- **Schema**: JSON 5.0 (Descriptions, CVSS scores, references)
- **Scope**: Software vulnerabilities within released products.
- **Differentiator**: CVE focuses on the *product vulnerability*, regardless of authorship method. AICI focuses specifically on the *authorship context* (AI generated code) and includes non-security failures.

### OSV (Open Source Vulnerability)
- **Maintainer**: Google / OpenSSF
- **Schema**: OSV schema format.
- **Scope**: Vulnerabilities within open source software packages.
- **Differentiator**: OSV aggregates vulnerabilities across OSS ecosystems. AICI encompasses all environments (including private enterprise code) and covers failures beyond traditional security vulnerabilities.

### AVID (AI Vulnerability Database)
- **Maintainer**: AVID ML
- **Schema**: AVID taxonomy.
- **Scope**: Vulnerabilities and harms inherent to AI models themselves (e.g., bias, misuse, data poisoning, hallucinations).
- **Differentiator**: AVID focuses on the *AI model*. AICI focuses on the *code generated by the AI tool*.

### AI Incident Database (AIID)
- **Maintainer**: Responsible AI Collaborative
- **Scope**: Harms caused by AI systems impacting end-users (e.g., autonomous vehicle crashes, biased hiring tools).
- **Differentiator**: AIID has a much broader scope focused on real-world harms. AICI is narrowly scoped to *code failures* produced by AI coding assistants.

### MITRE ATLAS (Adversarial Threat Landscape for AI Systems)
- **Maintainer**: MITRE
- **Scope**: Adversarial attacks mounted *against* AI systems.
- **Differentiator**: ATLAS documents attacks on AI. AICI documents failures where AI failed to produce safe/functional code.

### CWE (Common Weakness Enumeration)
- **Maintainer**: MITRE
- **Scope**: Categorization of software weaknesses and flaws.
- **Use Case in AICI**: AICI actively references CWE IDs to categorize the recognized pattern type of an incident, linking the "how" of the failure to established weakness types.

## Why AICI is Category Creating

1. **Authorship Focus**: The first database explicitly tracking incidents based on AI authorship.
2. **Operational Response**: AICI links incidents directly to actionable detection rules (e.g., Korext, Semgrep) allowing teams to prevent repeated incidents.
3. **Broad Definition of Failure**: Covers security breaches, severe compliance violations, and hallucinations causing production outages.

*Note: The AI Incident Registry Taxonomy and Schema are entirely original, designed from first principles with influence, but no copied text or unauthorized derivation, from the databases listed above.*
